Interest in IT

I have always been interested in video games, when I was in primary school, I got to own a Nintendo DS and I loved playing Yoshi’s Island, Pokemon Diamond and Super Mario Bros. The next console I owned was a PS3, which was when I spent most of my time playing games such as Uncharted and the Arkham series. To be able play a video game that I’ve made would be amazing and to know others have played it and shared that experience would be incredible. My dad has also inspired me to start a career in IT, having worked in the industry for many years. Currently, I do have a job working in IT that gets me to build computers and server racks and has me doing general patch management.

I chose to study at RMIT because I was told they have an excellent IT degree which prepares you very well to work in the industry and has lots of options when choosing something to specialise in. I also wanted to go to a university that was a lot closer and more accessible for me. Since previously that had made my life difficult going to and from the University. Because it was so difficult, I never found myself wanting to engage in any extracurricular activities or even wanting to go to uni.

I expect to know a couple of coding languages, specifically ones that are relevant to what I want to do and the ability to understand most code given time to look over them.

Ideal Job

DX solutions is offering a full-time position where I would lead a project through delivery to deployment. This includes writing and debugging software, talking to clients and the ability to analyse the software. The most exciting part of the job is to write code and to problem solve. These are the most important things that I would want in a job.

Experience required includes object-orientated programming in Javascript and C++, the ability to use programs such as mySQL, Apache and GIT. They also would like the ability to understand HTTP request/response cycle and integrate social media and other apps including Paypal.

I currently have a low-level understanding and programming skill in java and python and just began using GitHub. I also have experience with building and setting up servers and computers for businesses and can have experience with software testing.

Most of the experience can be attained at university, if not through classes I can take courses over the holidays to introduce myself to these programs and take time to learn them in my free time. By checking what my course has on offer I can take a pathway that compliments what I want to achieve.
